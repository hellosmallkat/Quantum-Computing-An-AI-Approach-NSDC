# Overview

This repository contains my implementation of the **National Student Data Corps (NSDC) Data Science Project: Quantum Computing – An AI Approach**.

The project explores the intersection of **artificial intelligence** and **quantum computing** by examining how quantum circuits can perform computations similar to those of classical neural networks. Rather than replacing traditional machine learning, quantum computing offers a new way to process information using qubits, superposition, and quantum gates.

Throughout this project, I learned how quantum mechanics can be applied to AI concepts by constructing quantum neurons, building simple quantum neural networks, and comparing their behavior with classical models.

# Key Takeaways

This project helped me better understand that quantum computing is fundamentally different from classical computing. Instead of manipulating binary bits, quantum algorithms operate on qubits that can exist in superpositions and are manipulated by quantum gates to perform computations.

By implementing quantum neurons and experimenting with parameterized quantum circuits, I gained a practical introduction to quantum machine learning and how AI concepts can be adapted to quantum systems. The project also strengthened my understanding of both classical neural networks and the mathematical intuition behind quantum computation.

# What I Learned

This project expanded my understanding of both artificial intelligence and quantum computing by showing how concepts from neural networks can be represented using quantum circuits. I learned how qubits, quantum gates, and parameterized rotations form the foundation of quantum machine learning while gaining hands-on experience programming with Qiskit.

Beyond learning the mechanics of quantum circuits, I developed a deeper appreciation for how emerging quantum technologies could complement traditional AI methods to solve future computational challenges.
